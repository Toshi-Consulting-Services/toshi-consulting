## Introduction to AI-powered QA Testing

The software testing landscape is undergoing a significant transformation. The integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Large Language Models (LLMs) is revolutionising the way tests are generated, executed, and maintained. At Toshi Consulting, we've seen firsthand how AI-powered QA testing can improve software quality, reduce testing time, and increase efficiency.

## Why AI in QA Suddenly Stopped Being Hype

In the last 18 months, AI in QA has transitioned from hype to reality. This shift is largely due to advancements in LLMs, agentic loops, and affordable inference. LLMs can now handle long-context understanding, enabling them to generate high-quality tests from existing code.

### From "AI Suggests Tests" to "AI Authors, Runs, and Maintains Them"

The evolution of AI in QA has been rapid. Initially, AI was used to suggest tests. Today, AI-powered tools can author, execute, and maintain tests. This shift has significant implications for testing teams, as it enables them to focus on high-value tasks.

### The Three Shifts: Long-Context LLMs, Agentic Loops, and Cheap Inference

The three key shifts driving AI adoption in QA are:

1. **Long-context LLMs**: LLMs can now handle long-context understanding, enabling them to generate high-quality tests from existing code.
2. **Agentic loops**: AI-powered tools can now execute and maintain tests, reducing manual effort and improving test coverage.
3. **Cheap inference**: Advances in hardware and software have made AI inference more affordable, enabling organisations to deploy AI-powered QA testing at scale.

## Where LLMs Genuinely Outperform a Human SDET

LLMs have demonstrated significant strengths in various testing tasks. Here are a few areas where LLMs outperform human SDETs:

### Unit-Test Generation from Existing Code

LLMs can generate high-quality unit tests from existing code, reducing manual effort and improving test coverage.

### Test Data Synthesis

LLMs can synthesise test data, including synthetic PII, reducing the risk of data breaches and improving test accuracy.

### Self-Healing Locators and Flaky-Test Triage

LLMs can help identify and fix flaky tests, reducing test maintenance effort and improving test reliability.

## Where LLMs Still Fail — and Why Your SDET Job Is Safer Than You Think

While LLMs have demonstrated significant strengths, they still have limitations. Here are a few areas where LLMs struggle:

### Exploratory and Adversarial Testing

LLMs struggle with exploratory and adversarial testing, requiring human creativity and critical thinking.

### Hallucinated Assertions and Confidently-Wrong Test Logic

LLMs can generate hallucinated assertions and confidently-wrong test logic, requiring human review and validation.
