## Why "what does IT consulting cost in India" is the wrong question

The honest answer to "what does IT consulting cost in India" is: it depends on four things, and any vendor quoting you a single rate is hiding three of them.

It depends on **which service line** — AI integration, blockchain, QA automation and digital marketing price very differently, and treating them as one undifferentiated "IT consulting" bucket is how buyers end up paying brand-identity rates for a smart contract audit. It depends on **which engagement model** — fixed-bid, time and materials, dedicated pod or monthly retainer — because each one prices risk, scope ambiguity and bench capacity differently. It depends on the **hidden line items** baked into the rate card, of which GST 18% and TDS 10% under Section 194J are only the two most predictable. And it depends on **buyer-side facts** — your DPDP exposure, your in-house capacity, your funding runway — that any serious quote has to interrogate before it can be sized.

We answer all four below, in rupees, with the ranges that match what we and our peers actually charge in 2026.

## The four engagement models — and what each one actually costs

Almost every IT consulting engagement in India is one of four shapes. The names are stable; the economics behind them are not, and most of the cost surprises buyers report later trace back to picking the wrong shape for the wrong scope.

### Fixed price — when it works, when it is a trap

A fixed-bid engagement converts a defined scope into a single rupee number, paid against milestones. It works beautifully when the scope is genuinely well-defined and the duration is short: a brand identity refresh (₹1.5-12L), a smart contract audit (₹3-25L), a 6-week AI proof of concept against a single dataset (₹3-15L). It is a trap when the scope is ambiguous and the vendor refuses to charge for a paid discovery phase — the vendor will load 20-35% risk premium into the quote, then bill change requests on top when the inevitable scope clarifications arrive. If you are looking at a fixed-bid quote longer than 8 weeks with no separate discovery line item, assume the cheque is 25% bigger than it reads on paper.

### Time and materials — the default for anything longer than 8 weeks

T&M bills against actual hours at agreed rates, typically ₹800-6,000/hr by seniority (junior engineer ₹800-1,500, mid ₹1,500-2,800, senior ₹2,800-4,500, architect or principal ₹4,500-6,000+). A serious T&M MSA includes a not-to-exceed cap per sprint or per month and a weekly burn report — without those two clauses, T&M becomes an open chequebook. It is the cheaper and lower-risk shape for any engagement where the destination is clear but the path is not, which is most non-trivial software work.

### Dedicated pod — the most cost-efficient shape above ₹4L/mo of spend

A dedicated pod is a ring-fenced team of 3-5 engineers, a lead, and a fractional architect or designer, billed at a flat monthly rate (typically ₹4-12L/mo all-in) on a 6-12 month commitment. Per blended hour it lands 25-35% cheaper than equivalent T&M, because the vendor amortises bench risk and ramp time across the longer commitment. The trade-off is buyer-side discipline: a pod without a clear product owner on your side will deliver expensive ambient work. Past roughly ₹4L/mo of consulting spend on a single workstream, the pod model is almost always the right shape — see [our outsource-vs-in-house decision framework](/blog/outsource-vs-in-house-tech) for when it tips over into a full-time hire.

### Retainer or fractional advisory — strategy and governance, not delivery

A retainer (₹1.5-3L/mo for a single senior consultant, ~30-40 hrs/mo) buys you a CTO, head of QA or head of marketing on a fractional basis: roadmap design, vendor management, hiring panels, board prep. It is the wrong shape for delivery-heavy work, and the right shape for a Series-A founder who needs a senior brain in the room for three meetings a week and is not yet ready to hire one at ₹80L base.

## The hidden line items every Indian invoice will carry

These are the line items that turn a ₹2.5L/mo retainer into a ₹3.1L cheque. They are not vendor dishonesty; they are statutory or operational facts that proposals routinely omit because nobody wants the headline number to grow.

**GST at 18%** applies to every IT consulting invoice in India under HSN 9983 / 998314. If your business is GST-registered, this is recoverable as input tax credit — a cash-flow cost, not a real cost. If you are not (early-stage LLP, foreign-incorporated entity billing into India, etc.), the 18% is a real cost. Ask the vendor to itemise GST separately and confirm input-credit eligibility before signing. The [official GST services schedule](https://cbic-gst.gov.in/gst-goods-services-rates.html) is the canonical reference.

**TDS at 10% under Section 194J** applies to professional and consulting fees above ₹30,000/year per vendor. You withhold it at source, deposit it with the government, and the vendor reclaims it against its annual tax liability. It is a cash-flow line item even when it is fully recoverable for the vendor, and most early-stage buyers fumble it on their first invoice. The [Income Tax India explainer of Section 194J](https://incometaxindia.gov.in/Tutorials/15-%20TDS%20on%20professional%20fees.pdf) is the source we cite in our own MSAs.

**Infra pass-through** covers everything the engagement consumes that is not a person-hour: LLM API tokens, cloud spend, BrowserStack or Sauce Labs seats, Figma seats, Mixpanel, Sentry, BugSnag, design assets, stock photography. Typical range is ₹15k-1.5L/mo depending on stack. The honest billing pattern is at-cost; the lightly-marked-up pattern is cost plus 10%; the predatory pattern is a flat "infra fee" that exceeds the underlying spend. Ask for the AWS, OpenAI and tooling invoices on request.

**Change-request markup** is the line item that has burned more Indian buyers than any other. Insist on a documented CR rate (typically 15-25% over the baseline T&M rate, or a flat ₹/hour for in-scope-adjacent work) inside the MSA, not as a verbal understanding. A vendor that resists documenting CR rates pre-signature is telling you exactly how the renegotiation will go three months in.

**Ramp, handover and exit costs** are almost never quoted upfront. Expect 4-8 weeks of half-productive billing while a new pod ramps onto your codebase (this is real — even excellent engineers cannot context-switch faster than that), and 2-4 weeks of dedicated handover billing at engagement exit. A vendor that promises full velocity from week one is either lying or planning to charge you for the ramp under a different label.

**Travel and on-site days** load ₹15-30k/day for senior consultants for any client requiring in-person workshops outside the vendor's base city. Panchkula or Gurugram clients asking for an in-person sprint review every fortnight need to budget this; remote-first engagements do not. The DPDP Act 2023 has also raised the bar on data-handling diligence — see [the MeitY DPDP portal](https://www.meity.gov.in/data-protection-framework) — and DPDP-compliant vendors generally price 5-10% above non-compliant peers because the compliance overhead is real.

> **Key insight:** GST plus TDS plus infra pass-through plus CR buffer typically add 25-32% to the headline number in year one. A ₹4L/mo retainer is a ₹5L cheque every month. Build the math into the board deck before the kickoff, not after the first invoice.

## When IT consulting is the wrong line item on your P&L

Some of the highest-value calls we have with prospects end with us telling them not to hire us yet. Three scenarios where consulting is genuinely the wrong line item.

**You do not have a defined business problem, only a buzzword.** "We need AI" and "we need blockchain" are not business problems. A discovery sprint can sometimes turn them into one, but if you cannot articulate, in one sentence, what changes for the business when the project ships, a consulting engagement will burn ₹15-30L producing a deck that confirms you should have answered that question first.

**Your data or process is so chaotic that 70% of consulting hours will be cleanup the vendor cannot productise.** Consultants charge ₹2,500-5,000/hr to do work that an internal ops hire can do at a fraction. If your CRM, finance and product data are not in a state where an external pod can ingest them in week one, fix that first — and consider whether an internal hire pays back faster than a retainer.

**You have a stable, predictable 18-month roadmap with funded budget.** The classic "build it in-house" case. A retainer is leverage during uncertainty; once the path is clear, an in-house lead at ₹60-90L base plus two engineers at ₹25-35L base each will outperform an equivalent-cost consulting pod on continuity and product depth. Our [outsource-vs-in-house decision framework](/blog/outsource-vs-in-house-tech) walks the math in detail.

> **Watch out:** A vendor that cannot articulate the three scenarios in which you should *not* hire them is either inexperienced or selling. The disqualifier is the trust move; without it, the rate card is just marketing.
