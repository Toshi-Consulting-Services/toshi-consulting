## What "decentralised KYC" actually means (and what it doesn't)

Decentralised KYC refers to the use of blockchain technology and decentralised identity (DID) standards to create a secure, transparent, and efficient KYC process. It involves the use of verifiable credentials and digital identities to verify customers' identities.

### Permissioned consortium chain vs. public chain (and why public is off the table for Indian PII)

In India, decentralised KYC solutions are built on permissioned consortium chains, such as Hyperledger Fabric or Polygon Supernet, which ensure the security and integrity of sensitive customer data. Public chains are not suitable for Indian PII due to regulatory requirements.

### Verifiable Credentials + DIDs in plain English

Verifiable credentials are digital certificates that prove a customer's identity, while DIDs are unique digital identifiers that enable secure and decentralised identity management.

### How it sits *on top of* CKYCR, V-CIP, and DigiLocker — not instead of them

Decentralised KYC solutions complement existing KYC processes, including CKYCR, V-CIP, and DigiLocker. They do not replace these processes but rather enhance them with a secure and efficient decentralised layer.

## Compliance — RBI, DPDP, SEBI, IRDAI lined up against the architecture

Decentralised KYC solutions must comply with Indian regulations, including the RBI's KYC Master Direction, the DPDP Act 2023, and SEBI and IRDAI guidelines.

### RBI Master Direction KYC (Jun 2025 amendments) and CKYCR upload obligations

The RBI's KYC Master Direction and CKYCR upload obligations require businesses to maintain accurate and up-to-date KYC records.

### DPDP Act consent receipts — where decentralised KYC actually wins

The DPDP Act 2023 requires businesses to obtain explicit consent from customers for data processing. Decentralised KYC solutions can help businesses meet this requirement.
