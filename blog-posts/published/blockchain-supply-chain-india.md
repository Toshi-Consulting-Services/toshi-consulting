## Blockchain in supply chain: 5 real implementations from India (and what we learned watching them)

The buzz around blockchain in supply chain management has been growing, but how does it actually work in practice? We've seen many market research reports and vendor product pages, but what about real-world implementations? At Toshi Consulting, we've analyzed five notable blockchain supply chain projects in India, and we're excited to share our findings.

## Why we're writing about implementations, not "potential"

The search engine results page (SERP) is dominated by market research reports and vendor product pages. However, these sources often lack concrete examples and real-world outcomes. We wanted to go beyond the hype and explore actual blockchain supply chain implementations in India.

## The five Indian implementations worth studying

### 1. Coffee Board of India + Eka Plus — blockchain e-marketplace for 350k+ growers

In 2019, the Coffee Board of India launched a blockchain-based e-marketplace for coffee growers. This platform, built on a permissioned blockchain, enables growers to directly sell their produce to buyers, eliminating intermediaries. [Talk to us](/contact) to learn more about this implementation.

> **💡 Key insight:** By using blockchain, the Coffee Board e-marketplace has reduced transaction costs and increased transparency for growers.

### 2. Tech Mahindra + StaTwig "VaccineLedger" — vaccine cold-chain traceability

Tech Mahindra partnered with StaTwig to implement VaccineLedger, a blockchain-based solution for vaccine cold-chain traceability. This solution has been deployed in Telangana, Maharashtra, and Arunachal Pradesh, and has shown significant improvements in vaccine supply chain efficiency. [Learn more about our blockchain consulting services](/services#blockchain-consulting).

### 3. NITI Aayog + GS1 India + Oracle — pharma drug authentication pilot

NITI Aayog, GS1 India, and Oracle collaborated on a pharma drug authentication pilot using blockchain. This project aimed to ensure the authenticity and track-and-trace of prescribed transport conditions for pharmaceuticals.

### 4. Telangana & Chandigarh — land-records blockchain pilots

The governments of Telangana and Chandigarh have launched blockchain-based pilots for land records. These projects aim to create a tamper-proof asset registry, improving land ownership transparency and reducing disputes.

### 5. UNDP Accelerator Lab + Spices Board + GS1 India — spice traceability

The UNDP Accelerator Lab, Spices Board, and GS1 India have collaborated on a spice traceability project using blockchain. This initiative focuses on quality assurance, trading, and supply chain transparency for spices.

## The pattern across all five — what worked, what stalled

While each implementation had its unique challenges and outcomes, some common patterns emerged. Clear regulator involvement, permissioned ledger use, and narrow product scope were key factors in successful implementations.

> **⚠️ Watch out:** Multi-stakeholder onboarding, mobile UX at the farm/last-mile, and integration with legacy ERPs were common challenges.

## When blockchain beats a regular database in an Indian supply chain

Blockchain is not a replacement for traditional databases, but rather a complementary solution for specific use cases. When you have four or more distrusting parties, a regulator, or an auditor involved, blockchain can provide a secure and transparent solution.

## The compliance wrapper — DPDP, RBI (if BFSI-adjacent), and data residency

The DPDP Act 2023 requires personal data to be stored in India. When implementing blockchain in supply chain, it's essential to consider data residency and use hash-only on-chain and off-chain data storage for compliance.

## A realistic adoption path for an Indian SME or mid-market manufacturer

To implement blockchain in supply chain, start by mapping your paper-based traceability process. Then, choose your stack — Hyperledger Fabric, Polygon Supernet, or managed Oracle/IBM — and pilot one SKU with 4-6 nodes.
