The concept of blockchain-based Know Your Customer (KYC) has been gaining traction in India, particularly in the fintech sector. As we navigate the complexities of KYC regulations and the potential benefits of blockchain technology, it's essential to understand the current landscape and future prospects.

## The 2026 Reality Check — What Actually Happened to "Blockchain KYC India"

In the past, initiatives like BankChain and Clear-Chain promised to revolutionize KYC processes using blockchain technology. While these pilots showed promise, they ultimately stalled due to various challenges. However, with the evolving regulatory landscape and advancements in technology, the time is ripe to reassess the potential of blockchain KYC in India.

### A Brief History of Blockchain KYC in India

In 2017, BankChain, a consortium of Indian banks, launched a blockchain-based KYC platform. The project aimed to create a shared, decentralized ledger for customer identities, reducing duplication and costs. Although the pilot was successful, it failed to scale due to regulatory and technical challenges.

### What Changed in 2023-2026

The introduction of the DPDP Act 2023 and updates to the RBI Master Direction on KYC have created a more conducive environment for blockchain-based KYC solutions. The RBI's increasing openness to "blockchain-based de-duplication" of CKYCR records has also sparked renewed interest in blockchain KYC.

## The Indian KYC Stack You Already Have (and Why It Matters)

Before exploring blockchain KYC, it's essential to understand the existing KYC infrastructure in India.

### Aadhaar e-KYC, Offline XML, and the Post-Puttaswamy Guardrails

Aadhaar e-KYC and offline XML have become essential components of India's KYC landscape. The Supreme Court's 2018 judgment on the Aadhaar Act provided clarity on the use of Aadhaar in KYC processes.

### CKYCR — What It Does Well, Where It Duplicates, Where It Leaks

The Central KYC Records Registry (CKYCR) is a centralized repository for customer KYC information. While CKYCR has reduced duplication, it still faces challenges related to data quality and leakage.

### Video-KYC (V-CIP), DigiLocker, and the Artefact-Storage Problem

Video-KYC (V-CIP) and DigiLocker have emerged as popular solutions for KYC and document storage. However, these solutions also raise concerns about data security and artefact storage.

## The Four Blockchain KYC Patterns We Actually See in 2026

We've identified four potential patterns for blockchain KYC in India:

### Pattern 1 — Permissioned Consortium Ledger for Shared KYC + AML/STR

This approach involves creating a shared, decentralized ledger for KYC and AML/STR reporting. [Talk to us](/contact) to learn more about this pattern.

### Pattern 2 — SSI / Verifiable Credentials on Top of DigiLocker and Sunbird RC

Self-Sovereign Identity (SSI) and verifiable credentials can enhance KYC processes by providing customers with control over their identity data.

### Pattern 3 — Private-Chain Audit Trail for Video-KYC Artefacts and Consent Receipts

This pattern involves using a private blockchain to create an audit trail for video-KYC artefacts and consent receipts.

### Pattern 4 — Tokenised Consent and Revocation Under DPDP

Tokenised consent and revocation can facilitate secure and transparent consent management, aligning with the DPDP Act 2026.

> **💡 Key Insight:** Blockchain technology can reduce KYC costs by up to 30% and improve processing times by 50%.

## The Regulatory Layer — RBI, PMLA, DPDP, and the Sandbox

The regulatory framework for blockchain KYC in India is evolving.

### What the RBI Master Direction on KYC Permits

The RBI Master Direction on KYC allows for the use of distributed ledger technology for record-keeping.

### PMLA Record-Keeping, STR Filing, and Where a Shared Ledger Helps vs Hurts

The Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA) requires robust record-keeping and STR filing. A shared ledger can facilitate compliance.

### DPDP as Data Fiduciary — Consent, Purpose Limitation, and the "Right to Erasure" vs Immutability Tension

The DPDP Act 2026 emphasizes the importance of consent management and purpose limitation. Blockchain technology can help address these requirements.

## Build, Join, or Skip — How We'd Think About It

When considering blockchain KYC, it's essential to evaluate the costs, benefits, and regulatory implications.

### When to Skip Blockchain Entirely and Fix Your CKYCR + V-CIP Plumbing Instead

For many fintechs, fixing their CKYCR and V-CIP plumbing is a more pressing priority than implementing blockchain KYC.

### When Joining an Existing Consortium is the Right Call

Joining an existing consortium can be a cost-effective way to leverage blockchain KYC.

### When Building a Private-Chain Audit Layer or SSI Overlay Pays Back

Building a private-chain audit layer or SSI overlay can provide enhanced security and transparency.

> **⚠️ Watch out:** Blockchain KYC is not a one-size-fits-all solution. It's essential to carefully evaluate the costs, benefits, and regulatory implications.
