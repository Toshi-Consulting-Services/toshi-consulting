## Why we're writing this
"B2B digital marketing India" is the natural hub query for any Indian tech buyer evaluating an agency, a fractional CMO, or building an in-house function. The SERP is crowded with agency-listicles ("Top 10 B2B agencies in India") and generic trend posts, but almost no one publishes a single, opinionated playbook that combines the Indian channel reality (LinkedIn + WhatsApp + SEO + intent data), honest ₹ benchmarks (CPC, CPL, retainer bands), and a build-vs-hire decision frame for tech founders. With Indian B2B e-commerce projected to hit ~$200B by 2030 and 78% of Indian businesses increasing digital budgets, this pillar captures commercial intent at the moment a CTO or founder asks "how do we actually do this?" — and it anchors every spoke post in our Digital Media cluster (LinkedIn ABM, content engine, SEO, WhatsApp Business, marketing-ops stack).

## Top 3 competing pages
1. https://www.balistro.com/b2b-digital-marketing-strategies-indian-companies-2026/ — "Top 10 B2B Digital Marketing Strategies for Indian Companies", ~2,500 words. Strong channel breadth (LinkedIn, SEO, content, ABM, WhatsApp) and India-specific framing. Weaknesses: listicle structure with no narrative through-line, no concrete ₹ CPL/CPC benchmarks, no honest discussion of which channels to skip, no DPDP/consent angle for cold outreach, and weak on measurement (pipeline contribution, attribution).
2. https://www.3mindsdigital.com/blog/b2b-digital-marketing-strategy-for-2026-what-actually-works/amp/ — "B2B Digital Marketing Strategy for 2026: What Actually Works", ~2,200 words. Good on the "70% of buyer research happens before sales contact" framing and content-funnel mapping. Weaknesses: globally framed (no Indian channel mix, no ₹), thin on ABM mechanics, no agency-vs-in-house guidance, no examples of failure modes, no FAQ structure for AI Overviews / featured snippets.
3. https://yoshmarcom.com/b2b-marketing-in-india-2026trends-storytelling-strategies-that-actually-win/ — "B2B Marketing in India 2026: Trends & Winning Strategies", ~2,000 words. Decent on storytelling and trust-building in the Indian context (case studies, regional credibility). Weaknesses: trend-piece tone (no implementable playbook), no budget ranges, missing technical-buyer channels (G2, GitHub, dev-focused communities, technical SEO), no DPDP/cold-email legal context, no measurement framework.

Our opening: a single argued essay (not a listicle) that picks the channels Indian B2B tech firms should actually run in 2026, gives honest ₹ benchmarks (LinkedIn CPC ₹150–₹650, MQL ₹1.5K–₹4K, retainer ₹50K–₹3L/mo), maps each channel to funnel stage, addresses DPDP-compliant cold outreach explicitly, and ends with a build-vs-hire-vs-hybrid decision frame. The differentiator is willingness to disqualify channels (e.g., "skip Instagram for enterprise SaaS", "don't run Google Display for ABM") instead of the typical do-everything listicle.

## Tone notes
- First-person plural ("we", "our clients") — Toshi voice throughout. We're an IT consulting firm that *runs* the Digital Media Marketing practice, not a pure agency reviewing the market from outside.
- Business-first paragraphs. Technical depth (attribution models, DKIM, intent-data signals) lives in 1–2 sentence asides or sidebars, never the main flow.
- All money in ₹ as default; convert to $ only when citing a global benchmark. Avoid "lakhs/crores" without numerals — use "₹2 Cr" or "₹20,00,000" consistently.
- Indian context as default: DPDP for compliance, RBI/MeitY for regulated verticals, Panchkula/Tricity/Gurgaon/Bengaluru in anonymised mini-examples. Global frameworks (HubSpot flywheel, Gartner CMO survey) cited where they sharpen the argument.
- Be willing to disqualify channels and budgets. The "channels to deprioritise" H2 and the sub-₹5L-ACV SEO carve-out should turn some readers away. That filters our inbound and earns trust with the rest.
- No hype words ("game-changing", "supercharge", "unleash", "10x your pipeline"). Concrete verbs: target, nurture, qualify, attribute, measure, disqualify.
- Pillar structure: every H2 should leave a thread that a cluster spoke can pick up. End each major section with "we go deeper on this in [internal link]" where the spoke exists, or flag "[forthcoming spoke]" where it doesn't yet.
- Close with a soft CTA — "book a free B2B marketing diagnostic" — not a hard pitch. Diagnostic gives us the same qualification value as a readiness audit on the AI side.
