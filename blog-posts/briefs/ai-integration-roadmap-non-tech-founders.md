## Why we're writing this
Non-tech founders are the loudest under-served buyer in India's AI market right now - 68% of small businesses are already using AI tools but "winging it" with no plan, and the head keyword "ai integration roadmap" hits exactly when they decide to stop winging it. The "90-day" framing matches a search pattern competitors have already validated (Presta, CIT, BayTech, Narratize all rank with it), and the "non-tech founders" qualifier turns a crowded head term into a defensible long-tail. For Toshi, this piece feeds the same audience as our enterprise pillar but at an earlier stage - founders who'll either hire us in month 3 or come back when they outgrow no-code tools. It also cleanly supports our AI Integration service page without sounding like a sales pitch.

Owns the non-tech founder angle and the "build an AI app" sub-intent. Missing: integration framing (focuses on building a new product, not integrating AI into an existing business), no 90-day cadence, US/SaaS-default examples, no compliance.
3. https://www.citsolutions.net/the-executives-90-day-ai-roadmap/ - "The Executive's 90-Day AI Roadmap", ~1,500 words. Strong on the executive/business-leader voice and Golden Triangle pilot-selection framework. Missing: non-tech founder specificity (skews mid-market exec), thin on tool selection, no India/INR, no honest disqualifier ("you don't need this yet"), almost no implementation detail beyond the framework.

Our opening: business-first ("here's the 90 days, in INR, with the three decisions you'll regret if you skip them"), the non-tech founder lens kept throughout (no "ask your CTO" cop-outs), explicit DPDP and Indian payments/GST realities, INR price bands for every tool class, and a Day 1 readiness check that disqualifies founders who should fix data/processes first - the exact filter our inbound pipeline needs.

## Tone notes
- First-person plural ("we", "our clients") throughout - Toshi voice.
- Talk to a founder, not a CTO. No jargon without a one-line plain-English gloss. If we say "RAG", we explain it in the next clause.
- Concrete ₹ numbers, not "it depends". Use ranges (e.g., "₹15K-₹40K/month for a no-code stack at this stage") and call out GST and INR billing realities for tools that bill in USD.
- Indian context as default - Panchkula/Tricity SME mini-examples (anonymised), Razorpay/Zoho/Tally as workflow anchors, DPDP not GDPR as the compliance frame.
- Be willing to disqualify - the readiness section should send some readers away. That's the point. It filters our inbound to founders who are actually ready.
- No hype, no "revolutionary", no "unlock". Verbs: pilot, measure, ship, kill, scale.
- Each phase ends with a one-line "you'll know you're on track if..." check the founder can self-grade against.
- Soft CTA at the end - a free 30-minute readiness call, not a sales pitch. The piece should be useful even for readers who never contact us.
- Avoid the listicle trap. This is a narrative roadmap with clear week-marks, not "12 AI tools for founders".
