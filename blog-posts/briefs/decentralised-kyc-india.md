## 4. Three questions a Toshi buyer will ask (FAQ)

1. **"Isn't CKYCR already a 'decentralised' KYC registry — why would I add blockchain on top?"**
   (Answer arc: CKYCR is *centralised reuse* operated by CERSAI; it solves duplication but not consent, auditability, or cross-sector portability. A decentralised layer adds (a) cryptographic consent receipts that satisfy DPDP, (b) verifiable credentials that move beyond BFSI into telecom/gig/health, (c) immutable audit trail RBI inspectors actually like.)
2. **"What does a decentralised KYC pilot realistically cost an Indian NBFC or fintech in 2026?"**
   (Answer arc: ₹15-40L for a 3-6 month pilot on a permissioned chain (Hyperledger Fabric or Polygon Supernet) wrapping an existing V-CIP + CKYC stack; ₹60L-1.5Cr for a production multi-party consortium; ongoing ₹2-6L/mo run cost. Headline saving: ₹40-150 per repeat customer onboarding vs. ₹500-1500 for fresh V-CIP.)
3. **"Will RBI and DPDP actually allow this — or am I going to build something I can't operate?"**
   (Answer arc: yes, within bounds. RBI hasn't published a decentralised-KYC circular, but the June 2025 amendments + DPDP consent framework + existing CKYCR rails make a permissioned, regulator-readable consortium fully compliant. Public/permissionless chains for PII are still a non-starter — we'll say so plainly.)
