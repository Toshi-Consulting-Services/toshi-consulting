## 1. Why we're writing this

`hire ai consultant india` sits at the bottom of the AI buyer funnel — the searcher has already accepted they need outside help and is now in vendor-evaluation mode with a budget. Capture this query and we capture meetings, not just pageviews. The SERP is dominated by directory listings (Clutch, Upwork, Freelancer) and self-serving "hire us" pages from competing agencies; there is no honest, India-specific decision guide written from the buyer's perspective, which is exactly the gap a 12-point checklist post should occupy.

## 4. Questions readers want answered

1. **"How do I tell a real AI integration consultant apart from an agency that just renamed its old web-dev team 'AI experts'?"** — The AI-washing problem. Needs concrete red flags: vague case studies, no live production references, can't name the LLM provider or the evals they ran, only talks about chatbots, can't explain RAG vs fine-tuning in plain English.
2. **"What should I actually pay an AI consultant in India — by hour, by project, by retainer — and where is the value-for-money sweet spot?"** — Needs honest ₹ price bands: discovery/audit (₹50K–₹2L), fixed-bid PoC (₹3L–₹15L), monthly retainer (₹1.5L–₹8L), enterprise integration (₹15L–₹1Cr+), and the trade-offs of each model.
3. **"What goes in the contract — IP, data residency, DPDP liability, model weights, prompts, handover — so we're not held hostage at the end of the engagement?"** — Needs the clauses Indian buyers routinely miss: data-processing addendum, sub-processor disclosure, India-region hosting, work-for-hire IP assignment, prompt and fine-tune ownership, source-code escrow, 90-day post-engagement support, and the DPDP fiduciary-vs-processor question.

## 5. Outline (H2/H3)

### H2: Before you hire anyone — the 2-question gut check
- H3: Do you have a problem AI actually solves, or are you chasing a buzzword?
- H3: Is your data in a state a consultant can do something with in 90 days?
- Callout (sidebar): "Skip the consultant if…" — three scenarios where the right answer is fix-data-first or buy-SaaS-instead, not hire-a-firm.

### H2: The 12-point checklist (scoreable, one discovery call)
> Each item presented as **Ask → Good answer → Red flag**. The 12 are the spine of the article; group them as four pillars of three points each so the post reads as an argued framework, not a listicle.

- H3: Pillar 1 — Proof of real AI work (points 1–3)
  - 1. Show me one live production deployment with real users (not a demo, not a GitHub repo)
  - 2. Which LLM provider, which evals, which guardrails — name them
  - 3. Walk me through one project where the model failed and what you did about it
- H3: Pillar 2 — Fit with your business and stack (points 4–6)
  - 4. Have you integrated AI with our category of system (ERP, CRM, custom app)?
  - 5. Who actually does the work — and can I meet them before signing?
  - 6. What does the first 30 days look like, and what do I get at the end?
- H3: Pillar 3 — Indian regulatory and commercial fit (points 7–9)
  - 7. How do you handle DPDP — consent capture, PII redaction, audit logs?
  - 8. Where will our data live (India region, US LLM endpoints, self-hosted)?
  - 9. GST, TDS, milestone invoicing — is your contract Indian-buyer-ready?
- H3: Pillar 4 — How the engagement ends (points 10–12)
  - 10. Who owns the code, the prompts, the fine-tunes, the model weights?
  - 11. What is the handover plan — documentation, training, runbooks?
  - 12. What happens 90 days after go-live when something breaks?

### H2: What should this actually cost in India? (real ₹ ranges)
- H3: Discovery / readiness audit — ₹50K–₹2L (1–3 weeks)
- H3: Fixed-bid PoC — ₹3L–₹15L (4–10 weeks)
- H3: Monthly retainer (fractional AI team) — ₹1.5L–₹8L/month
- H3: Enterprise integration / multi-system rollout — ₹15L–₹1Cr+ (3–12 months)
- Callout (insight): A hard rule — **walk away from anyone who quotes a fixed bid before a paid discovery phase.** Fixed bids before scoping push risk back onto you.

### H2: The contract clauses Indian buyers routinely miss
- H3: IP assignment — code, prompts, fine-tunes, evals, all of it in writing
- H3: Data-processing addendum + DPDP fiduciary-vs-processor classification
- H3: Sub-processor disclosure (OpenAI, Anthropic, Azure, AWS Mumbai) and the audit-trail obligation
- H3: Source-code escrow + 90-day post-engagement support window
- H3: Exit clause — your data, your model, your account credentials, in 14 days

### H2: Consultant, freelancer, agency, or in-house — pick the right shape
- H3: When a solo freelancer is enough (one bounded use-case, < ₹5L budget)
- H3: When an AI consulting firm earns its margin (multi-system, compliance, change management)
- H3: When you should hire in-house instead (predictable AI roadmap, ≥ 18 months of work, ability to attract senior ML talent)
- H3: The hybrid we recommend most often — consulting firm for the first integration, in-house ownership from year two

### H2: The questions we'd want you to ask us
> Short, candid section. We list five questions we'd be happy to answer on a discovery call — pricing transparency, references, DPDP posture, handover plan, walk-away terms. Earns the trust that converts.

## 8. Tone notes

- First-person plural "we" throughout — Toshi's voice. Where we say "we walk clients through this exact checklist in our first discovery call," it should feel true, because it is.
- Business-first, technical depth as proof. The CTO and the non-tech founder should both finish the post nodding. Lead each checklist point with the business consequence, not the technical detail.
- Honest about ourselves. This is a peak-conversion post, but we name our own category and explicitly acknowledge we are one of the firms the reader is evaluating. The "questions we'd want you to ask us" section is a trust-builder, not a humblebrag — keep it short and concrete.
- Indian context as default — ₹ figures inline (not in footnotes), DPDP as a first-class checklist pillar, GST/TDS mention in the contract section, no apology for assuming the reader files Indian tax returns.
- Willing to disqualify. The "skip the consultant if…" sidebar is critical — it filters the inbound to the leads we can actually serve and earns the trust of everyone else.
- No emojis, no hype words ("revolutionary", "unleash", "game-changing"). Verbs: integrate, audit, redact, hand over, escrow, measure.
- Concrete > generic at every paragraph. Each of the 12 checklist points must include one "red flag" sentence that the reader can apply in a discovery call this week.
- Close with a soft CTA — "if you want a second opinion on a vendor you're already evaluating, book a fit call" — not a hard pitch. Buyers in this query state are allergic to closing pressure.
