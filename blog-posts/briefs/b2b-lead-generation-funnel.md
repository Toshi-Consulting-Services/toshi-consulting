## 1. Why we're writing this

`b2b lead generation funnel` is a mid-funnel commercial query — the searcher already has a website, runs some ads or content, and is now trying to understand *why pipeline isn't moving*. They are weeks (not months) away from either rebuilding the funnel in-house or hiring an agency. Indian SERPs for this term are dominated by US SaaS blogs (HubSpot, Salesforce, Cognism) writing for $50K ACV and 12-month enterprise cycles, plus thin "12 lead-gen hacks" listicles from Indian agencies that never name a stage conversion rate. There is no India-specific, numbers-honest, 90-day operator's guide written from a firm that actually runs the full stack (paid + SEO + content + sales-ops) for B2B clients. That is the gap a cluster post under our Digital Media pillar should occupy — and it carries direct commercial intent into our `/services#digital-media` and `/contact` pages.

## 4. Questions readers want answered

1. **"What does a realistic B2B lead-gen funnel look like in India — what conversion rate should I expect at each stage, and how do I know whether my numbers are normal or broken?"** — Needs concrete India-context benchmarks: visitor → lead 1–3%, lead → MQL 25–40%, MQL → SQL 30–50%, SQL → opportunity 40–60%, opportunity → close 20–35%, with the honest caveat that a ₹5L deal at 1.5% visitor-to-close is healthy and a ₹50K deal at 0.5% is not.
2. **"Where in the funnel am I actually losing pipeline, and what do I fix first?"** — Needs a leak-diagnostic that walks the reader stage-by-stage with the question to ask at each (Is traffic right-fit? Is the form too long? Is response time > 1 hour? Is lead scoring lying? Is the proposal slow?), plus the single highest-ROI fix at each leak.
3. **"Can I really build this in 90 days, or is that marketing-blog fiction?"** — Needs an honest 90-day plan broken into three 30-day sprints (audit + plumbing, content + capture, nurture + closing-loop), with what realistically gets built vs what is still in progress at day 90, and the team shape (1 marketer, 1 SDR, 1 part-time content writer, 1 ops tool) it actually takes.

## 8. Tone notes

- First-person plural "we" throughout — Toshi's voice. Sentences like "we run this 90-day rebuild for clients in exactly this shape" must read true because they are.
- Business-first, technical depth as proof. The founder reads it for the funnel logic; the marketing lead reads it for the conversion benchmarks and the WhatsApp/DPDP detail. Both should nod.
- Numbers in every section. No stage description without a benchmark range, no follow-up tactic without a time window, no recommendation without a ₹ figure. Vague is the failure mode of every competing post — refuse it.
- Indian context as default — ₹ inline (not in footnotes), DPDP as a first-class stage-3 concern (not an afterthought), WhatsApp Business API as the assumed Indian follow-up channel, GST/TDS only mentioned if the proposal-stage subsection drifts into pricing.
- Willing to disqualify. The "if you're under ₹1L/month, fix offer and follow-up first" sidebar and the "you don't need an agency yet if…" close are critical — they filter inbound to leads we can actually serve and earn trust with the rest.
- Honest about ourselves. We are a Digital Media services firm writing a digital media funnel guide — name it once, early, then let the post's quality do the selling. The closing CTA is a "funnel teardown," not a "book a sales call."
- No emojis, no hype words ("explode your pipeline", "10x leads", "growth hack"). Verbs: capture, qualify, score, hand over, nurture, close, audit, instrument.
- Concrete > generic at every paragraph. Each of the 7 stages must include one named tool, one benchmark number, and one failure mode the reader can spot in their own funnel this week.
- Close with a soft CTA — "if you want a second pair of eyes on your funnel, send us your last 90 days of GA4 + CRM exports and we'll run the diagnostic" — not a hard pitch. Mid-funnel commercial searchers walk away from pressure.
