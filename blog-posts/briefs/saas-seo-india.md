## 1. Why this post (and why now)

"SaaS SEO India" is a Low-Medium head term (est. 100-400 searches/mo), but the surrounding cluster — *SaaS SEO strategy*, *SaaS content marketing India*, *programmatic SEO SaaS*, *product-led SEO*, *B2B SaaS keyword research* — pulls combined intent into the 1.5-3k/mo range with high commercial follow-through. The searcher is almost always a founder, growth lead, or marketing head at a Series-Seed-to-B SaaS company who has tried generic SEO advice and bounced off it because SaaS SEO is a different sport: long buyer journeys, free-trial conversions, integration/comparison/alternative pages, and AI-overview cannibalisation are not what a typical "SEO checklist" article handles.

Crucially, 2026 is the inflection year for two shifts that no Indian top-ranking page covers honestly: (a) **AI Overviews + ChatGPT Search are eating top-of-funnel SaaS traffic** — the playbook now has to optimise for inclusion in generative answers, not just blue links; and (b) **the DPDP Act compliance moment** is forcing Indian SaaS to rethink cookie-based attribution, GA4 consent mode, and form-side data capture. We can own this gap.

This post is the first cluster piece feeding the pillar `/blog/b2b-digital-marketing-india` (post #39) and the natural top-of-funnel feeder to `/services#digital-media-marketing-branding`. The reader's next question after "what's the checklist" is "who can run this for me" — that's our handoff.

## 4. Three questions a Toshi buyer will ask (FAQ)

1. **"How long before SaaS SEO actually shows pipeline impact in India?"**
   (Answer arc: 6-9 months to first qualified trial signups from organic, 12-18 months to compounding traffic; faster only if you already have domain authority or buy your way in via digital PR.)
2. **"Does SEO still matter when ChatGPT and Google AI Overviews answer the question directly?"**
   (Answer arc: yes, but the goal shifts — you optimise to be *cited* inside generative answers, not just to rank. Structured data, original data/benchmarks, and crisp definitional paragraphs are now first-class ranking assets.)
3. **"What's the minimum monthly SEO budget for an early-stage Indian SaaS to take this seriously?"**
   (Answer arc: ₹1.5-3L/mo covers a credible in-house+freelancer stack — content lead + technical SEO retainer + link-building budget. Below that, focus on one BOFU page and paid channels instead.)
