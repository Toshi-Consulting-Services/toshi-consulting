Because the visitor is already in "select a vendor" mode, this post is a direct conversion path into `/services#blockchain-consulting` and `/contact`.

## 4. Three questions a Toshi buyer will ask (FAQ)

1. **"What should a blockchain consultant in India actually cost — hourly, project, and retainer?"**
   (Answer arc: senior consultant ₹3,500-9,000/hr; production pilot ₹8-25L; full platform ₹25L-1.5Cr; retainer ₹1.5-6L/mo. Below ₹2,000/hr is a junior dev, not a consultant — name the gap honestly.)
2. **"Does the firm understand FIU-IND, the 30% VDA tax, and DPDP — or are they going to leave me to fight regulators alone?"**
   (Answer arc: any firm touching tokens, exchanges, wallets, NFT or Web3 *must* know FIU-IND AML/CFT (Jan 2026 update), PMLA Principal Officer/Designated Director appointment, 30% + 1% TDS, and how DPDP "right to erasure" collides with on-chain immutability — typically solved via off-chain PII + on-chain hash.)
3. **"Who owns the code, the keys, and the data after the engagement ends?"**
   (Answer arc: insist on full IP assignment on payment, source escrow, multi-sig admin key handover, exportable data, no vendor-locked SaaS components in the critical path. This is where 70% of blockchain engagements fail at exit.)

**H1: Hiring a Blockchain Consulting Firm in India: The Questions to Ask Before You Sign**

- **H2: Why hiring a blockchain consultant is harder than hiring a web team** (~150 words)
  Frame: irreversible decisions (immutable ledger, public-chain costs, regulator exposure) mean a bad partner is expensive to unwind. This post is the checklist we wish every client used on us.

  - H3: "Do you actually need a blockchain?" — if the firm does not push back, walk away. Most use-cases are better served by a Postgres audit log.
- **H2: Strategy & fit — 3 questions** (~250 words)
  - H3: Can you show me one project where you talked a client *out* of blockchain?
  - H3: Which chain would you pick for my use-case, and why not the other three? (Public L1 vs. L2 vs. permissioned Hyperledger/Corda)
  - H3: What does your discovery phase look like, and what is the deliverable?
- **H2: Technical depth — 3 questions** (~300 words)
  - H3: Who writes our smart contracts, and which audit firm signs them off? (Name-check CertiK, Trail of Bits, OpenZeppelin, Halborn, Quantstamp.)
  - H3: How will you handle key management and admin multi-sig? (Hardware wallets, MPC, threshold signatures.)
  - H3: How do you reconcile on-chain immutability with the DPDP Act's right to erasure? (Off-chain PII + on-chain hash; zero-knowledge for sensitive fields.)
- **H2: Regulatory — 3 questions every Indian buyer must ask** (~300 words)
  - H3: Are you up to date on the FIU-IND AML/CFT guidelines updated 8 Jan 2026? (Travel Rule, Principal Officer, 5-year records.)
  - H3: How do you handle the 30% VDA tax + 1% TDS in product flows? (TDS deduction at source, exchange integration, accounting hooks.)
  - H3: Have you taken a project through the RBI Regulatory Sandbox or worked with a partner bank on a permissioned-chain pilot?
- **H2: Commercial — 3 questions that protect your P&L** (~300 words)
  - H3: What does this cost in INR, broken into discovery / build / audit / deploy / 12-mo run? (Realistic 2026 bands: ₹8-25L pilot, ₹25L-1.5Cr platform, ₹3.5-9k/hr senior consultant.)
  - H3: Fixed-bid, T&M, or hybrid — and how are scope changes priced?
  - H3: Who owns the IP, the repo, the keys, and the data on day 1, day 90, and after exit? (Insist on assignment-on-payment + source escrow + multi-sig handover.)
- **H2: Post-launch — 3 questions about the year after go-live** (~200 words)
  - H3: What is your SLA for a smart-contract vulnerability disclosure?
  - H3: How do you handle chain forks, gas-price spikes, or L2 sequencer outages?
  - H3: What is your exit / handover plan, and how long do you support a successor team?

## 8. Tone notes

- First-person "we" throughout — written as a peer consultant telling the buyer what we would ask if we were sitting on their side of the table.
- Open with the buyer's risk, not our service catalogue. The post earns the right to pitch only in the final H2.
- INR everywhere; never quote in dollars. Lakhs / crores spelled Indian-style (₹8-25L, ₹1.5Cr).
- Each of the 12-15 questions follows the same micro-format: **The question → why it matters → what a good answer sounds like → the red-flag answer**. This rhythm is the post's structural moat over the generic listicles.
- Name names where it builds trust: FIU-IND, RBI Regulatory Sandbox, DPDP Act, CertiK, OpenZeppelin, Hyperledger Fabric, Polygon, Corda. Vague = competitor; specific = us.
- Honest > optimistic. Include the "talk you out of blockchain" line — that is the single most powerful trust move on a commercial-intent page.
- Technical depth as proof, not performance. One short example of off-chain PII + on-chain hash for DPDP reconciliation is enough — do not write a tutorial.
- Soft CTA only. Two contextual links to `/services#blockchain-consulting` and one to `/contact` after the red-flags section. No banners, no "Book a call" CTAs mid-prose.
- Avoid hype words ("revolutionary", "disruptive", "unlock", "Web3 revolution"). The reader is in vendor-selection mode — they want a steady hand, not a pitch deck.
