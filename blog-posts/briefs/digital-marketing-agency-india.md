## 1. Why we're writing this

`digital marketing agency india` is a high-volume, peak-commercial-intent query — easily 8K-15K/mo on the exact match, with the supporting cluster (`best digital marketing agency in india`, `digital marketing services india`, `digital marketing company in india`, `hire digital marketing agency`, `top digital marketing agency india`) clearing 40K+/mo combined. Every searcher is either a founder who just lost patience with their in-house marketer, a CMO with a quarterly board commitment, or a startup that's burned its first agency and is on attempt two. This is the bottom of the funnel — meetings, not pageviews.

The SERP is a swamp. Page one is dominated by three patterns: (1) self-promotional "Top 25 agencies in India" listicles ranked by the publishing agency (Fruitbowl, upGrowth, themediaant, lapaone, ztrategize, techeasify) where the author conveniently appears in the top five; (2) directory pages (Clutch, DesignRush, Semrush Agency Partners) that monetise the buyer's confusion; (3) generic "red flags" posts (Aryan SS, repIndia, Adtric, Nucleo Analytics) that recycle the same eight bullets — "no transparent pricing", "guaranteed #1 ranking", "no case studies" — without telling the buyer what a *good* answer sounds like. None give honest ₹ price bands, none cover the 2026 Indian regulatory layer (DPDP consent capture, ASCI influencer-disclosure code, Google Consent Mode v2, GST/TDS on retainers, the new IT Rules notification standards), and none disqualify themselves from any reader.

Our wedge: the only India-anchored, scoreable buyer's checklist on the page, written by an agency willing to name the questions we'd want a prospect to ask *us*. Twelve questions with good-answer / red-flag rubrics, real ₹ ranges by business size and service mix, a DPDP/ASCI/consent-mode compliance section nobody else writes, and a sidebar that tells 20% of readers they shouldn't be hiring an agency yet. That last move is what earns the trust that converts the other 80%.

## 4. Questions readers want answered

1. **"How do I tell a real performance agency apart from a logo farm that subcontracts everything to freelancers and reports vanity metrics?"** — Needs concrete tells: case studies with ROAS / CPA / organic-session numbers (not screenshots of follower counts), named pod members the buyer can meet before signing, transparent attribution methodology, willingness to show one campaign that *failed* and what they did about it, separate disclosure of media spend vs. management fee.
2. **"What should I actually pay a digital marketing agency in India in 2026 — by service, by business size, and where is the value-for-money sweet spot?"** — Needs honest ₹ bands: SEO ₹25K-1L/mo (startup) to ₹1L-3L/mo (competitive verticals like SaaS, fintech, ecomm); PPC management 10-20% of ad spend or ₹15K-50K/mo floor; social ₹20K-80K/mo; full-funnel retainer ₹1.5L-5L/mo (SMB) to ₹5L-15L+/mo (mid-market/enterprise). The trade-offs of each model (retainer vs project vs performance-fee vs hybrid) and a hard rule on minimum ad-spend floors for meaningful PPC results.
3. **"What goes in the contract and the compliance posture — DPDP consent on lead forms, ASCI on influencer briefs, Consent Mode v2 on GA4, GST/TDS on invoices, IP on creatives — so we don't inherit liability when the engagement ends?"** — Needs the clauses Indian buyers routinely miss: data-processing addendum naming the agency as processor (not fiduciary), consent-capture audit on every form the agency builds, ASCI-compliant influencer disclosure clause in scope-of-work, server-side tagging + Consent Mode v2 setup, work-for-hire IP assignment on all creatives and copy, ad-account ownership reversion (the buyer owns the Google/Meta accounts — not the agency), 30-day data-export clause, and the GST input-credit + 10% TDS u/s 194J reality on retainer invoices.

### H2: The 12-question checklist (scoreable, one discovery call)
> Each question framed as **Ask → Good answer → Red flag**. Grouped into four pillars of three questions, so the post reads as an argued framework, not a listicle.

- H3: Pillar 1 — Proof the work is real (questions 1-3)
  - 1. Show me one case study with ROAS, CPA, and organic-sessions numbers from the last 12 months in our category
  - 2. Walk me through one campaign that underperformed — what did your attribution model show and what did you change?
  - 3. Who in your team will actually run our account, and can I meet them on a working call before signing?
- H3: Pillar 2 — Fit with your business, channel mix, and scale (questions 4-6)
  - 4. Have you scaled paid acquisition past our current monthly spend in our category (D2C, SaaS, BFSI, B2B services, ecomm)?
  - 5. What's your point of view on channel mix for a business at our stage, and what would you *not* do?
  - 6. What does the first 60 days look like — audit, baseline, first creative tests, first reporting cadence?
- H3: Pillar 3 — Indian regulatory and commercial fit (questions 7-9)
  - 7. How do you handle DPDP consent on lead forms and the data-processing addendum?
  - 8. Are your influencer briefs ASCI-compliant, and do you have a server-side Consent Mode v2 setup for GA4?
  - 9. How do GST input credit, 10% TDS u/s 194J, and milestone invoicing show up on your contract?
- H3: Pillar 4 — How the engagement ends (questions 10-12)
  - 10. Who owns the ad accounts, the creatives, the copy, the keyword lists, the audience lists, and the tracking implementation?
  - 11. What's the 30-day exit plan — data export, account handover, runbook, knowledge transfer?
  - 12. What happens if a Google or Meta algorithm change wipes out the campaign you scaled — who eats the loss and how do we re-baseline?

### H2: What should this actually cost in India? (real ₹ bands by service and size)
- H3: SEO — ₹25K-1L/mo (early-stage), ₹1L-3L/mo (competitive verticals — SaaS, fintech, ecomm)

### H2: The compliance layer Indian agencies routinely skip (2026 reality)
- H3: DPDP — lead-form consent capture, audit logs, processor vs fiduciary classification, the ₹250 cr penalty exposure
- H3: ASCI — influencer disclosure code, paid-partnership tagging, the agency's brief must build it in

- H3: IT Rules + intermediary obligations on ad creatives, plus ad-platform community-standards risk
- H3: GST input credit, 10% TDS u/s 194J on retainer invoices, milestone-based payments
