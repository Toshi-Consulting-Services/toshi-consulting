# Topic Brief: AI integration case study — process automation in fintech

## 1. Why this topic (and why now)

The head phrase "ai integration case study" is a lower-volume, higher-intent query — searchers are almost always buyers in evaluation mode (founder, CFO, ops head, fractional CTO) looking for proof a vendor has actually shipped, not theory. Volume on the exact phrase is modest (sub-500/mo globally per SERP shape), but the cluster around it — "ai in fintech case study", "ai process automation fintech", "ai fintech ROI" — carries combined demand in the 1-3K/mo range and pulls a very different SERP: vendor case-study libraries (Automation Anywhere, IBM, RTS Labs, SoluLab, iLink) and a thin layer of trade-press explainers. None of the top results are a single, narrative, India-context case study with honest ₹ numbers and DPDP/RBI overlays.

Three reasons we proceed:

1. **RBI's FREE-AI framework landed in 2026** — the "tolerant supervisory stance for first-time errors plus mandated governance" posture is reshaping every Indian fintech AI roadmap. A case study that bakes FREE-AI compliance into the build is timely and uncited by global competitors.
2. **The commercial intent is unusually clean.** Anyone typing "ai integration case study" + a fintech qualifier is shopping. We win by being the case study with concrete ₹ numbers, a named (anonymised) Indian NBFC/PA/lending client, and a 90-day timeline a buyer can actually budget against.
3. **Toshi has the proof.** This is the post that converts the AI Integration service page — it pairs naturally with the pillar (#1) and the cost post (#5) as the "we've actually done this" anchor of the cluster.

**Verdict: proceed.** Treat as a credibility post rather than a top-of-funnel volume play. Optimised for assisted conversion (the lead reads this *after* the pillar) and for being cited in AI-overview answers to "who has built AI process automation for an Indian fintech".

## 4. Questions a Toshi buyer would ask (FAQ entries)

1. **"What did the AI integration actually cost end-to-end, and when did it pay back?"** — Need an itemised ₹ build cost (discovery + integration + model work + governance), monthly run cost (LLM/infra/observability), and the months-to-payback math, with the assumptions stated so the buyer can re-run it for their own volumes.
2. **"How did you keep this RBI- and DPDP-compliant — especially under the new FREE-AI framework?"** — PII redaction at ingestion, India-region hosting, model cards / risk register, audit-log retention, human-in-the-loop on every irreversible action, and how the "tolerant first-error" posture changes (does not eliminate) governance work.
3. **"What broke that wasn't in the original plan, and how would we avoid it?"** — Data-quality gaps in legacy systems, drift after the first rate-policy change, the change-management work with the ops team, the vendor lock-in moment when a foundation-model price shifted. The honest "lessons" section is the whole point of the post.

## 8. Tone notes

- First-person plural ("we", "our team", "we'd do differently") throughout — this is a Toshi war story, not a vendor brochure.
- Narrative arc beats listicle. The reader should feel the timeline: week 1 discovery, week 6 first phase live, month 4 drift incident, month 5 payback. Dates and weeks are the spine.
- Anonymise the client convincingly but honestly. Use a composite-style framing ("a mid-sized NBFC we worked with in 2025-26, AUM in the ₹500-1,500 cr band") rather than fake-specific names. Note up front that some numbers are rounded for confidentiality.
- One concrete number per H3 minimum. ₹, %, weeks, STP rate, TAT — vague paragraphs get cut.
- Indian context is the whole point. RBI / DPDP / FREE-AI mentioned naturally; ₹ inline; vendors named (Azure India region, AWS Mumbai, OpenAI vs self-hosted Llama) with the reasoning we actually used.
- Honest about what broke. The "lessons" H2 is non-negotiable and must include at least one mistake we own (data-quality under-budgeting, late observability). Buyers trust the post that admits the messy middle.
- Avoid: "in today's fast-paced world", "revolutionary", "game-changing", "leveraging cutting-edge", any sentence that could be in any vendor's brochure. No emojis.
- Hard target: 1,950–2,100 words. TL;DR box at the top is non-negotiable for AI-overview pickup. FAQ block at the end is non-negotiable for FAQPage schema.
- Close with a soft CTA (the 90-day template + contact) — this is a credibility post, so the CTA earns the right to exist by being useful first.
