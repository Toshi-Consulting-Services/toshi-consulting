## 4. Three questions a Toshi buyer will ask (FAQ)

1. **"What's a realistic monthly retainer for an IT consulting partner in India in 2026 — and what do I actually get for it?"**
   (Answer arc: ₹1.5-3L/mo for a fractional advisory engagement (1-2 senior consultants, ~30-40 hrs/mo, strategy + light delivery oversight); ₹4-8L/mo for a small embedded pod (3-5 mixed-seniority engineers + a lead, 100-160 hrs/mo, real delivery); ₹10-25L/mo for a full multi-disciplinary pod across AI/QA/marketing for a Series-A scaling phase. Anything below ₹1L/mo is either a single freelancer billed as a "firm" or a junior body-shop arrangement — calibrate expectations accordingly.)
2. **"How do I compare a fixed-price quote against a T&M quote when the scope is the same on paper?"**
   (Answer arc: add 20-35% buffer to any fixed-price quote that does not include a paid discovery phase — the vendor has priced their risk into your invoice. T&M with a not-to-exceed cap and weekly burn reports is usually the cheaper *and* lower-risk shape for any project longer than 8 weeks or with >20% scope ambiguity. Fixed-price wins only when the deliverable is genuinely well-defined and short (≤6 weeks, e.g. a brand identity refresh or a smart contract audit), or when the buyer's procurement system literally cannot process a variable invoice.)
3. **"What hidden line items will I see on the first invoice that weren't in the proposal?"**
   (Answer arc: 18% GST on Indian invoices (refundable as input credit if you're GST-registered, a real cost if you're not), 10% TDS withholding under Section 194J on consulting fees, infra pass-through (LLM API credits, cloud, BrowserStack, Figma seats — typically ₹15k-1.5L/mo depending on stack), change-request markups (15-25% over baseline rates on out-of-scope work), travel and on-site days for any Panchkula/NCR client requiring in-person workshops, and vendor exit / handover costs in the final 30 days. Budget 10-20% on top of the headline retainer for these in year one.)

- **H2: Why "what does IT consulting cost in India" is the wrong question to start with** (~150 words)
  Frame: the price depends on which service line (AI integration, blockchain, QA, digital marketing — they price very differently), which engagement model (fixed, T&M, pod, retainer), and which hidden line items the vendor has or hasn't pre-loaded into the rate card. We're going to answer all four in INR, not a single hand-wavy hourly band.

- **H2: The four engagement models — and what each one actually costs in India** (~450 words)
  - H3: Fixed price — when it works, when it's a trap (₹2-50L per scope; +20-35% hidden risk premium when there's no paid discovery)
  - H3: Time & materials — the default for anything longer than 8 weeks (₹800-6,000/hr by seniority, with a not-to-exceed cap and weekly burn reports)
  - H3: Dedicated pod — the most cost-efficient model above ₹4L/mo of spend (3-5 person mixed-seniority team at ₹4-12L/mo all-in, 6-12 month commitments, ~25-35% cheaper per hour than equivalent T&M)
  - H3: Retainer / fractional advisory — the right shape for strategy + governance, not delivery (₹1.5-3L/mo for senior advisory, ~30-40 hrs/mo)

- **H2: The 2026 INR price bands, by service line** (~500 words)
  - H3: AI integration consulting (Discovery ₹50k-2L, fixed-bid PoC ₹3-15L, dedicated AI pod ₹6-15L/mo, enterprise rollout ₹25L-2Cr+)

- **H2: The hidden line items every Indian invoice will include (and why your proposal didn't mention them)** (~350 words)
  - H3: GST 18% (input-creditable for registered businesses, real cost otherwise)
  - H3: TDS 10% under Section 194J on consulting fees (cash-flow impact even if recoverable)
  - H3: Infra pass-through (LLM tokens, cloud, BrowserStack, Figma, Mixpanel, etc. — typically ₹15k-1.5L/mo, billed at cost or at cost + 10%)
  - H3: Change requests (15-25% premium on out-of-scope work — *insist on a documented CR rate in the MSA before signing*)
  - H3: Ramp, handover, and vendor-exit costs (4-8 weeks of half-productive billing on ramp; 2-4 weeks of dedicated handover at exit)
  - H3: Travel + on-site days (₹15-30k/day loaded for senior consultants — relevant for Panchkula/NCR clients requesting in-person workshops)

- **H2: A worked example — 12-month consulting budget for a Series-A SaaS in Gurugram** (~250 words)
  Concrete worked total in INR: 1 fractional CTO advisory retainer (₹2.5L/mo × 12 = ₹30L), 1 embedded AI integration pod for Q2-Q4 (₹8L/mo × 9 = ₹72L), 1 QA automation framework build in Q3 (fixed-bid ₹12L), 1 brand refresh + performance marketing retainer Q1-Q4 (₹3.5L/mo × 12 = ₹42L). ## 8. Tone notes

- First-person plural "we" throughout — Toshi is the consultant doing the budget math alongside the buyer, *and* one of the firms they may end up evaluating. This dual posture must be acknowledged once, early in the post, then disappear into honest reporting.
- Peak-conversion intent — but the reader is highly sceptical. Calm specificity wins. Every rate band must have a low end, a high end, and a sentence explaining what moves a buyer from one to the other.
- INR everywhere, Indian-style (₹2.5L/mo, ₹1.8-2.1 Cr, not "$30k/mo" or "$2.4M"). Use lakhs and crores consistently; no decimal rupees, no USD parenthetical conversions in the prose (one footnote-style mention is enough).
- Show your work. Every total in the worked example must reconcile to its line items in a table the reader can copy into their own board deck. The honesty is the differentiator and the trust-builder.
- Service-line discipline. Each of the four Toshi service lines gets its own price band paragraph — not a single blended "IT consulting" rate card. This is what no competitor does and what every serious Indian buyer needs.
- Engagement-model discipline. Fixed price, T&M, dedicated pod, retainer — each gets its own H3 with a clear "when this model is right / wrong" sentence. Avoid the temptation to recommend the retainer just because it's our highest-margin shape.
- Name the hidden line items. GST 18%, TDS 10% (Section 194J), infra pass-through, change-request markup, ramp, handover, vendor exit, travel. Every buyer has been burned by at least one of these and will recognise themselves in the section — that recognition is the conversion moment.
- Include the "when consulting is the wrong line item" H2. This is the trust move every competitor skips. Disqualifying 20% of inbound is the cheapest filter Toshi has.
- One short, candid disclosure of Toshi's own pricing bands in the closing H2. Not a brochure, not a hard sell — just the same transparency we've spent 1,800 words demanding from other vendors.
- DPDP appears once, substantively, in the hidden-line-items section. RBI only if a BFSI sub-example is used in the worked example. GST and TDS appear with their official statutory references because rate-card claims without statutory anchors lose credibility.
- No hype ("world-class", "trusted partner", "end-to-end", "best-in-class", "seamless"). The reader is doing budget math under board pressure; calm, specific, INR-denominated honesty wins both the click and the call.
- Soft CTA only. One link to `/services`, one to `/contact`, both near the end. The worked-example table does the closing work; the CTA just opens the next door.
