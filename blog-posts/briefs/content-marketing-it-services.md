## 8. Tone notes

- First-person plural ("we publish", "our editorial calendar", "what's working for us") — Toshi voice. This post is unusual because Toshi *is* the case study; lean into it without bragging.
- **Show real numbers wherever defensible** — even rough bands ("our top 5 posts drive ~70% of inbound demos", "case studies convert at 3-4x the rate of listicles for us") beat generic "content works" claims. If a number is sensitive, give a ratio instead of an absolute.
- Quantify spend in **₹** — the budget question is the single biggest reason Indian IT founders bounce off generic Western content guides. Title doesn't promise rupees but the body should deliver them in the budget section.
- Business-first paragraphs; technical depth (schema, internal-link graphs, GEO citation mechanics) lives in 1-2 sentence asides or single-line examples, not lectures.
- Indian context as default — DPDP-era trust signals, LinkedIn-India dynamics, festive-season publishing rhythm. Global frameworks (E-E-A-T, GEO) cited where they add weight, never for show.
- **Be willing to disqualify** — the "what we tried and killed" section is the credibility play. Naming gated PDFs and AI-generated filler as wasted spend is what separates this from the 42-expert round-ups.
- No hype words ("crush it", "10x your traffic", "game-changer"), no emojis. Concrete verbs: published, clustered, repurposed, decommissioned, measured.
- One mid-article internal link to a live cluster (AI Integration pillar) as proof + one soft CTA to the Digital Media service at the close. The post itself is the credentials.
- Keep to ~1,800 words: 9 H2s at ~180 words average, with the 90-day plan and case-study sections taking the heavier word-count. Resist scope creep into general SEO or paid-ads territory — this is the content-marketing-specific tactical post.
