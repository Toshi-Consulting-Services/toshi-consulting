## 1. Why this post (and why now)

"Performance marketing budget" sits in a Medium-volume keyword cluster (head term ~500-1.2k searches/mo globally; combined with allocation / India / benchmarks / B2B variants the cluster clears 3-5k/mo). Intent is informational but commercial-adjacent: the searcher is a founder, marketing head, or CFO trying to defend or rebuild next quarter's paid plan. SERPs are dominated by US-centric agency blogs (WordStream, HawkSEM, Single Grain) quoting CPL ranges in dollars and a long tail of Indian agencies (WebFX-clones, Webdura, Schbang explainers) repeating "7-10% of revenue" without showing channel-mix math.

Nobody on page one is doing what an Indian SME or D2C operator actually needs in 2026: **a rupee-denominated channel-by-channel allocation table** that accounts for (a) the Google + Meta duopoly tax, (b) the rise of CTV / connected-audio / regional creators, (c) DPDP-era attribution loss (server-side conversions, consent mode v2), and (d) GST input-credit treatment on ad spend. That is our wedge. The post also seeds future commercial pieces (CAC-payback calculator, MMM-for-SMEs, WhatsApp + paid social funnels) and links cleanly into `/services#digital-media-marketing-and-branding`.

## 4. Three questions a Toshi buyer will ask (FAQ)

1. **"What % of revenue should an Indian SME or D2C brand spend on performance marketing in 2026?"**
   (Answer arc: 7-12% of revenue for growth-stage D2C, 4-7% for B2B services, 12-20% for pre-PMF startups in customer-acquisition mode; with 55-70% of that going to Google + Meta and the rest split across creative production, MarTech, and experimentation.)
2. **"How do I split a ₹10 lakh per month performance budget across Google, Meta, WhatsApp, and CTV?"**
   (Answer arc: typical balanced split — ~40% Google (Search + PMax), ~35% Meta (Advantage+ + Click-to-WA), ~10% creative production, ~7% MarTech / tracking / consent infra, ~5% YouTube / CTV upper-funnel, ~3% always-on experimentation. Tune by channel-level MER, not last-click ROAS.)
3. **"How much of my budget gets eaten by DPDP / consent-mode / tracking loss in 2026?"**
   (Answer arc: expect 8-15% effective CPL inflation from consent-mode opt-outs and signal loss; mitigate with server-side GTM (~₹8-25k/mo hosted), enhanced conversions, and a documented DPDP consent flow. Treat this as a budget line, not an accident.)

## 8. Tone notes

- First-person "we" throughout — we are the consultant the CFO calls before signing off the quarterly plan.
- Lead with the buyer's P&L and growth stage, not the channel menu. Allocation is a function of business stage, not platform fashion.
- INR everywhere; quote budgets in lakhs and crores (₹10L/mo, not "$12k/mo"). Use USD only in parenthetical context when citing a global benchmark.
- Indian context is a feature: name DPDP, GST input credit on ad invoices (18%), WhatsApp / Click-to-WA, CTV (JioCinema, Hotstar), regional creators, ShareChat / Moj where relevant.
- Honest > optimistic. Include a paragraph on **when NOT to increase budget** (broken funnel, weak LP, CAC-payback > 18 months) — every competitor skips this.
- Treat tables as the deliverable. The rupee allocation table for ₹2L / ₹10L / ₹50L budgets is the single artefact a reader will screenshot — make it copy-paste defensible.
- Technical depth as proof, not performance: one paragraph on server-side GTM cost, one on consent-mode v2 signal loss, one on PMax feed quality — enough to show we have built these, not enough to lose a non-tech founder.
- Soft CTA only. Lead reader to `/services#digital-media-marketing-and-branding` and `/contact` once, near the end.
- Avoid hype words ("crush", "explode", "10x", "growth-hacking"). The reader is defending a budget to a CFO; calm, sourced, rupee-denominated numbers earn the trust.
