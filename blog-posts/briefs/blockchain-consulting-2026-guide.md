## Why we're writing this
"Blockchain consulting services" is exactly where serious buyers land in 2026 - past the crypto-hype cycle, looking for partners who can scope tokenisation, smart-contract audits, supply-chain pilots and KYC infrastructure without burning eight months on a vendor pitch. The global market is projected at USD 5.92B in 2025 with a ~25% CAGR through 2035 (Spherical Insights), and the conversation has visibly shifted from "what is blockchain?" to "how do we comply with GENIUS Act / MiCA and ship at scale?". For Toshi this is the PILLAR of our Blockchain cluster - it has to hub all 11 sibling posts (smart-contract audits, tokenisation, KYC, supply-chain, MVP cost, hiring, RBI/DPDP compliance, public-vs-private, healthcare records, decentralised KYC, platform choice) and capture commercial intent with India-specific framing nobody else is doing well.

## Tone notes
- First-person plural ("we", "our blockchain team", "we've shipped") throughout - Toshi voice, not third-person consultancy-speak.
- Business-first paragraphs; technical depth (Merkle trees, gas optimisation, EIP references) lives in 1-2 sentence asides and the occasional sidebar, never the main flow.
- Use Indian context as default - ₹ figures, RBI/SEBI/DPDP references, FIU-IND, Tricity client mini-examples (anonymised) - while still citing global standards (NIST, ISO/TC 307, MiCA, GENIUS Act) where they apply.
- This is the PILLAR - it should read as a single argued essay with chapter breaks, not a listicle. Each H2 should be a defensible position, not a roundup.
- Be aggressively willing to disqualify. The readiness filter must say out loud: "If you don't have a multi-party trust gap, you don't need blockchain - use Postgres with signed receipts and save ₹40L." That earns trust and filters our inbound to serious buyers.
- No crypto-bro vocabulary ("HODL", "to the moon", "diamond hands", "WAGMI"). No hype words ("revolutionary", "game-changing", "unleash", "disrupt"). Concrete verbs: audit, tokenise, settle, govern, custody, integrate.
- Distinguish clearly between blockchain (the technology) and crypto/VDAs (the asset class) - non-tech founders conflate them and competitors don't bother correcting it.
- End with a soft CTA to a free 30-min readiness call, not a sales pitch. Offer the readiness filter as a downloadable one-pager.
- Word target 2900-3100. Pillar is broad-but-deep - aim for one strong opinion per H2 rather than encyclopaedic completeness.
