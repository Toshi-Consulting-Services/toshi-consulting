## Introduction to B2B Lead Generation Funnel
We've seen many Indian B2B firms struggle to convert website traffic into qualified pipeline. The issue isn't a lack of leads, but rather a leaky funnel that fails to nurture and convert them. A well-designed B2B lead generation funnel can help you identify and fix these leaks, resulting in a significant increase in qualified opportunities and closed deals. In this post, we'll outline a 7-stage B2B lead generation funnel that works for Indian firms, including WhatsApp follow-up, DPDP compliance, and leak diagnostics.

## Why Most Indian B2B Funnels Don't Work
Most Indian B2B funnels don't work because they're designed with a wrong understanding of the customer journey. They focus on generating more leads, rather than nurturing and converting them. The traffic-without-pipeline trap is a common issue, where lead generation increases, but qualified opportunities remain flat. There are four root causes for this issue: wrong ICP (Ideal Customer Profile), wrong offer, slow follow-up, and no nurture. If you're under ₹1L/month in marketing spend, fix your offer and follow-up before touching the funnel.

## The 7-Stage B2B Funnel
The 7-stage B2B funnel is designed to address the issues mentioned earlier. It includes:
- Stage 1: Right-fit visitor (1-3% convert to lead)
- Stage 2: Lead captured (form, WhatsApp, email, demo request)
- Stage 3: Marketing-qualified lead / MQL (25-40% of leads)
- Stage 4: Sales-qualified lead / SQL (30-50% of MQLs)
- Stage 5: Opportunity (40-60% of SQLs)
- Stage 6: Proposal sent (60-80% of opportunities)
- Stage 7: Closed-won (20-35% of opportunities)

> **💡 Key Insight:** A well-designed B2B funnel can increase qualified opportunities by 30-50% and closed deals by 20-35%.

## The 90-Day Rebuild
Rebuilding your B2B funnel in 90 days requires a structured approach. Divide the process into three 30-day sprints:
- Days 1-30: Audit + plumbing
- Days 31-60: Content + capture
- Days 61-90: Nurture + closing loop

## The Leak Diagnostic
To identify leaks in your funnel, ask the following questions at each stage:
- Stage 1: Is traffic right-fit?
- Stage 2: Is the form too long or too short?
- Stage 3: Is lead scoring accurate?
- Stage 4: Is the handover from marketing to sales smooth?
- Stage 5: Is the discovery call effective?
- Stage 6: Is the proposal template effective?
- Stage 7: Is the post-close loop effective?

> **⚠️ Watch out:** Leaks in your funnel can result in a significant loss of qualified opportunities and closed deals. Identify and fix them as soon as possible.

## The Indian Follow-up Stack
The Indian follow-up stack includes WhatsApp, email, and call. WhatsApp is the highest-converting channel for Indian B2B follow-up, with a DPDP-compliant consent pattern. The 5-touch sequence we use includes:
- Call within 1 hour
- WhatsApp within 4 hours
- Email same-day
- Value-add email day 3
- Breakup email day 7

## DPDP-Compliant Lead Capture
DPDP-compliant lead capture requires explicit consent from the lead and a clear opt-out mechanism. Use a one-checkbox consent pattern, plain Hindi-English language, and a link to a real privacy policy.

## Build in-House or Hire a Partner
Building a B2B funnel in-house can be effective if you have a marketer who can write and pull GA4 data, a founder willing to be in weekly funnel reviews, and a budget of ₹3L/month or more. However, if you don't have these resources, hiring a partner can be a better option.
