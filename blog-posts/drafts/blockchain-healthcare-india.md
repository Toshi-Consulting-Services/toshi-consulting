## Blockchain for Healthcare Records in India — What Actually Works in 2026

The healthcare sector in India is undergoing a significant transformation with the integration of technology. One of the key areas of focus is the management of electronic health records (EHRs) through blockchain technology. We've seen a growing interest in blockchain healthcare India, particularly with the implementation of the Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM). This mission aims to create a national digital health ecosystem, and blockchain is playing a crucial role in it.

## The Honest Starting Point — What ABDM Already Solves

The Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM) has made significant strides in creating a national digital health ecosystem. It provides a Health ID, Health Professional Registry (HPR), and Health Facility Registry (HFR), along with a consent manager. These components form a federated EHR backbone, making it easier to manage healthcare records. Our team has built solutions that integrate with ABDM, ensuring seamless data exchange and management.

## Where Blockchain Genuinely Adds Value in Indian Healthcare

While ABDM provides a robust framework, blockchain technology adds value in specific areas:

### Tamper-Evident Audit Trails for High-Stakes Records

Blockchain ensures the integrity and authenticity of high-stakes records, such as those related to oncology or organ transplants. By providing a tamper-evident audit trail, blockchain helps maintain the trustworthiness of these records. We've implemented blockchain solutions for clients in the healthcare sector, ensuring the security and integrity of their data.

### Multi-Party Insurance Claims Adjudication

Blockchain facilitates multi-party insurance claims adjudication by providing a transparent and secure platform for all parties involved. This includes hospitals, third-party administrators (TPAs), insurers, and the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI). Our team has developed blockchain-based solutions for claims adjudication, reducing disputes and increasing efficiency.

### Pharma Supply Chain and Drug Traceability

Blockchain helps in ensuring the authenticity and quality of pharmaceuticals by tracking them throughout the supply chain. This is particularly important in India, where counterfeit drugs are a significant problem. We've worked with clients to implement blockchain-based solutions for drug traceability, ensuring the safety and efficacy of pharmaceuticals.

## Where Blockchain Is the Wrong Tool (and a Regular Database Wins)

While blockchain has its advantages, it is not the solution for every healthcare problem. For instance:

### General OPD Records — ABDM's Federated Model Is Enough

For general outpatient department (OPD) records, ABDM's federated model is sufficient. Blockchain would be overkill and more expensive than necessary.

### Real-Time Imaging / Large File Storage — Chains Hate Big Payloads

Blockchain is not suitable for storing large files, such as medical images. The technology is not designed to handle big payloads, and it would be more efficient to use a regular database.

### Single-Hospital Internal EHR — No Multi-Party Trust Problem to Solve

For internal EHR management within a single hospital, blockchain is not necessary. The trust problem that blockchain solves is not relevant in this scenario.

> **💡 Key Insight:** Blockchain is not a replacement for existing healthcare systems but a complementary technology that adds value in specific areas. Its use cases are narrow but critical, such as tamper-evident audit trails and multi-party claims adjudication.

## The Compliant Architecture Pattern for India

For blockchain to be effective in Indian healthcare, it must comply with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 (DPDP Act) and integrate with ABDM. This means:

### Permissioned Chain (Hyperledger Fabric / Indus / Polygon Supernet) — Not Public Ethereum

Permissioned chains, such as Hyperledger Fabric, are more suitable for healthcare applications than public Ethereum. They offer better security, scalability, and compliance with regulations.

### Hash on-Chain, Encrypted PII Off-Chain — The DPDP Right-to-Erasure Fix

To comply with the DPDP Act, personal identifiable information (PII) should be stored off-chain in encrypted form, while only hashes are stored on-chain. This ensures data privacy and the right to erasure.

### ABDM-Compliant Integration via HL7 FHIR + Consent Artefacts

Integration with ABDM requires the use of HL7 FHIR and consent artefacts. This ensures seamless data exchange and management.

> **⚠️ Watch out:** Implementing blockchain in healthcare requires careful consideration of regulatory compliance, scalability, and integration with existing systems. Failure to address these challenges can lead to project failure.
