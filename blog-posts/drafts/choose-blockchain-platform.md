## Introduction to Choosing a Blockchain Platform
Choosing the right blockchain platform is a crucial decision for Indian businesses, as it can significantly impact the success of their projects. With numerous platforms available, each with its strengths and weaknesses, the selection process can be overwhelming. In this guide, we will walk you through the key considerations, regulatory compliance, and cost factors to help you make an informed decision.

## Step 0 — Do You Actually Need a Blockchain?
Before selecting a blockchain platform, it's essential to determine if you actually need a blockchain. Ask yourself these four questions: Are there multiple parties involved who don't trust each other? Do these parties need to share write access to data? Is there a need for tamper-evidence? Is there no neutral intermediary available? If you answer 'yes' to all these questions, a blockchain might be the right choice. Otherwise, a regular database could suffice.

## The First Fork in the Road — Permissioned or Permissionless?
The first decision you need to make is whether to opt for a permissioned or permissionless blockchain platform. Permissioned blockchains, like Hyperledger Fabric, are suitable when you control who joins the network, whereas permissionless blockchains, like Ethereum or Polygon, are better when you need open access or composability. Consider your use case, regulatory requirements, and the level of control you need over the network to make a decision.

> **💡 Key Insight:** The Indian regulator's view on permissioned vs. permissionless blockchains is crucial. The RBI tolerates permissioned ledgers, but treats public-chain assets as VDAs, subject to 30% income tax + 1% TDS.

## The Six Criteria That Actually Decide It
When selecting a blockchain platform, consider the following six criteria:
* Use case fit: Does the platform align with your business needs?
* Throughput and finality: What are the transaction processing capabilities and confirmation times?
* Cost model in INR: What are the costs associated with building, running, and maintaining the platform?
* Developer ecosystem and hiring pool in India: Is there a sufficient talent pool to support your project?
* Regulatory and compliance surface: What are the regulatory requirements, and how will you ensure compliance?
* Vendor lock-in and exit cost: What are the risks of being tied to a particular vendor, and what are the costs of switching?

## The Platforms — A Plain-English Shortlist
Here's a brief overview of popular blockchain platforms:
* Ethereum: The safe default for permissionless, but expensive at L1.
* Polygon: The practical EVM choice for Indian startups, with cheap gas and an India team.
* Solana: High throughput, but with occasional outages.
* Avalanche subnets: Your own chain without running the consensus.
* Hyperledger Fabric: The enterprise/regulated default in India.
* Corda: Finance-first, used by Indian banks in IBBIC.

## The Decision Tree — 6 Questions to Land on a Platform
To simplify the selection process, use the following decision tree:
1. Are you in a regulated industry?
2. Do you need open membership or composability?
3. Do you require EVM tooling?
4. What are your expected transaction processing requirements?
5. Do you have an Indian dev team?
6. What is your compliance posture?
Answering these questions will lead you to one of the top blockchain platforms.

## Indian-Only Considerations Most Guides Skip
When selecting a blockchain platform, consider the following Indian-specific factors:
* Vishvasya stack and NBFLite sandbox: Free permissioned-network experimentation.
* DPDP: Keep personal data off-chain, store hashes only.
* VDA tax: 30% + 1% TDS applies to VDAs.
* IBBIC and bank-grade Corda networks for BFSI buyers.
