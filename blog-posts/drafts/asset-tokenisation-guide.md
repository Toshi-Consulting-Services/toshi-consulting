## Tokenisation Explained: Real Estate, Loyalty, Equity

As we navigate the evolving landscape of digital assets, our clients often ask us about tokenisation. What is it, and how can it benefit their businesses? In this **asset tokenisation guide**, we'll explore the concept, its applications in real estate, loyalty programs, and equity, and the regulatory considerations in India.

Tokenisation is the process of converting assets into digital tokens on a blockchain. This allows for fractional ownership, increased liquidity, and improved transparency. We've seen significant interest in tokenisation across various sectors, including real estate, loyalty programs, and equity.

In India, the regulatory environment is evolving, with SEBI and RBI playing crucial roles in shaping the tokenisation landscape. Our team has built a comprehensive understanding of the regulatory requirements, and we'll share our insights in this guide.

## The 60-Second Answer for a Board Meeting

For a board meeting, you need a concise overview of tokenisation and its applications. Here's a one-paragraph TL;DR:

Tokenisation converts assets into digital tokens on a blockchain, enabling fractional ownership and increased liquidity. We see three primary use cases: tokenised real estate, loyalty programs, and equity. Each has its benefits and regulatory considerations. For instance, tokenised real estate can provide a new fundraising avenue, while tokenised loyalty programs can enhance customer engagement.

Here's a one-line rule of thumb for each asset class:

* Tokenised real estate: feasible via SEBI-registered SM REIT and AIF wrappers.
* Tokenised loyalty: a low-risk entry point, doesn't trigger securities regulations.
* Tokenised equity: still inside the SEBI sandbox, not yet a public offering.

## What "Tokenisation" Actually Means (in Business Terms)

### Tokens, Smart Contracts, and Custody — Explained Without Jargon

Tokenisation involves creating digital tokens that represent ownership or rights to an underlying asset. Smart contracts are self-executing contracts with the terms of the agreement written directly into code. Custody refers to the secure storage and management of these tokens.

We've built a robust understanding of these concepts and their applications. Our team can help you navigate the technical aspects of tokenisation.

### Why This Is Not the 2017 ICO Story Repeating Itself

The 2017 ICO craze was marked by unregulated token sales, often without clear rights or protections for investors. In contrast, today's tokenisation is about creating compliant, permissioned systems with robust KYC and regulatory oversight.

## Use Case 1 — Tokenised Real Estate in India

### What's Legally Possible Today

In India, tokenised commercial real estate is feasible via SEBI-registered SM REIT (Real Estate Investment Trust) and AIF (Alternative Investment Fund) wrappers. This allows for fractional ownership and increased liquidity.

Our team has worked with clients to navigate these regulatory frameworks. We can help you understand the requirements and benefits of tokenised real estate.

### What a Pilot Looks Like

A tokenisation pilot involves creating a digital token representing ownership in a property. The pilot includes issuance, KYC'd whitelist, and secondary transfer.

> **💡 Key Insight:** A well-structured tokenisation pilot can reduce the time and cost associated with traditional real estate transactions.

### Where It Breaks

Challenges include FEMA regulations on foreign investors, stamp duty, and registrar coordination. Our team can help you navigate these complexities.
