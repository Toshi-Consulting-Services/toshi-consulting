## AI Integration Case Study: Process Automation in Fintech

The fintech industry is rapidly evolving, and companies are looking for ways to improve efficiency, reduce costs, and enhance customer experience. At Toshi Consulting, we've seen firsthand how AI integration can help fintech companies achieve these goals. In this case study, we'll share our experience with a mid-sized NBFC (Non-Banking Financial Company) that we helped automate processes using AI.

## The Client and the Problem

Our client was a mid-sized NBFC with an AUM (Assets Under Management) of ₹500 crores and a ticket volume of 10,000 transactions per month. They were facing challenges with manual processes, including loan document verification, payment reconciliation, and tier-1 support triage. These processes were time-consuming, prone to errors, and required a large workforce.

## The Challenge

The client's existing processes were bleeding margin due to high operational costs, slow turnaround times, and poor accuracy. Earlier RPA (Robotic Process Automation) attempts had plateaued, and the client was looking for a more efficient solution.

## How We Scoped It

Our team conducted a two-week discovery process to understand the client's requirements and identify opportunities for automation. We analyzed their existing processes, data quality, and technology infrastructure. We also reviewed their regulatory compliance requirements, including RBI and DPDP Act obligations.

## What We Shipped

We developed an AI-powered automation solution that consisted of three phases:

### Phase 1: Document IDP and Loan File Verification

We used Azure Document Intelligence to automate loan document verification, which reduced the processing time from 5 days to 1 day. We also developed a fine-tuned classifier to improve the accuracy of loan file verification.

### Phase 2: Reconciliation Agent

We built a reconciliation agent using LangGraph and Postgres, which automated the reconciliation process and reduced the turnaround time from 36 hours to 4 hours.

### Phase 3: Support Triage and Draft Reply

We developed a support triage and draft reply system using RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generator) over the policy KB, which automated tier-1 support triage and reduced the deflection rate from 23% to 58%.

## The Results

Our AI-powered automation solution achieved significant results, including:

* STP (Straight-Through Processing) rate on loan file verification: 41% → 78%
* Reconciliation TAT (Turnaround Time): 36 hrs → 4 hrs; FTE (Full-Time Equivalent) redeploy: 4 → 1 on the queue
* Tier-1 support deflection: 23% → 58%; CSAT (Customer Satisfaction) held flat

The client achieved a payback period of approximately 5 months, with a build cost of ₹18-22 lakhs and a monthly run cost of ₹1.4 lakhs.

## What We'd Do Differently Next Time

While the project was successful, there are always lessons to be learned. Here are a few things we'd do differently next time:

* We under-budgeted data-quality work by 30%, which led to some delays in the project.
* We should have wired observability in earlier to detect model drift.
* We should have kept a second-source plan for one of the components to mitigate vendor lock-in.
