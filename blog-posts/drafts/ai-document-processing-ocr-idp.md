## H2: Why "AI document processing" means three different things

AI document processing encompasses a range of technologies, including **Optical Character Recognition (OCR)**, **Intelligent Document Processing (IDP)**, and **LLM-based extraction**. Each of these technologies plays a distinct role in the document processing pipeline.

### H3: Layer 1 — OCR (pixels to plain text)

OCR is the foundational technology that converts scanned or photographed documents into machine-readable text. It's a critical step in document processing, but it has limitations. OCR can struggle with poor image quality, complex layouts, and handwritten text.

### H3: Layer 2 — IDP (text to structured, validated fields)

IDP takes OCR a step further by extracting structured data from documents, validating it, and integrating it into workflows. IDP uses a combination of machine learning algorithms and business rules to identify and extract relevant data fields.

### H3: Layer 3 — LLM extraction (fields plus reasoning over context)

LLM-based extraction uses large language models to extract data from documents and provide context-aware insights. This approach can handle complex documents and provide more accurate results than traditional OCR or IDP.

> **💡 Key insight:** AI document processing can improve accuracy and efficiency, but it's not a one-size-fits-all solution. Businesses must carefully evaluate their document processing needs and choose the right technology for their specific use case.

## H2: A short history: from Tesseract to Document AI to GPT-4o

The evolution of AI document processing has been rapid. From the early days of Tesseract OCR to the current era of Document AI and GPT-4o, the technology has improved significantly.

### H3: Why classical OCR plateaued at ~95% on real-world Indian documents

Classical OCR technology has limitations, particularly when dealing with poor image quality, complex layouts, and handwritten text. These limitations have led to the development of more advanced technologies like IDP and LLM-based extraction.

### H3: How transformer-based IDP closed the gap on layout and handwriting

Transformer-based IDP has improved the accuracy of document processing, particularly for complex layouts and handwritten text. This technology uses a combination of machine learning algorithms and business rules to identify and extract relevant data fields.

### H3: What multimodal LLMs added in 2024-2026 — and what they broke

Multimodal LLMs have added significant capabilities to AI document processing, including the ability to handle complex documents and provide context-aware insights. However, these models also introduce new challenges, such as hallucination and audit trail gaps.
